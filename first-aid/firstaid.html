Elbow

A figure-eight method to support the joint, reduce swelling, or protect a wound. The bandage should be snug but not fight, maintaining circulation while allowing limited movement. Commonly used for sprains, abrasions, or post-surgical care.

Eye

Involves placing a sterile pad over the closed eye and securing it without pressure to protect from injury, infection, or light exposure. Common after eye trauma or surgery.

Elevation Sling

An elevation sling supports and elevates the arm using a triangular bandage to reduce swelling and limit movement. Commonly used after arm or hand injuries.

Forearm

A spiral technique with crepe or gauze to support injuries like sprains or cuts, starting at the wrist and moving up to the elbow, providing compression while allowing hand movement and circulation.

Forehead

Applying a sterile dressing to the wound, securing it with a bandage wrapped around the head, ensuring no pressure is applied while keeping the eyes and breathing clear.

Armstring

Sling supports and immobilizes an injured arm by positioning it in a flexed position and using a triangular bandage to secure it around the neck, with the hand slightly above the elbow for elevation.

Palm

Covering the wound with sterile gauze and securing it with a snug bandage that extends from the palm to the wrist, ensuring comfort and circulation while preventing movement.

Head

Applying a sterile dressing to the wound and securing it with a bandage wrapped around the head, ensuring the eyes and ears are clear and that the bandage is not too tight.
